Mirror Case Study
Research Define Ideate Test
Mirror is an affordable brick-and-mortar clothing retailer for anyone looking for trendy clothes. With the rise of online shopping, they wanted to create their own online shopping experience to take advantage of the huge opportunity for sales as well as clear out the remaining inventory in their warehouses.

This project was assigned through the UX Academy of Design Lab, in which I was responsible for research, UX/UI design, and asset creation.

Goals & Objectives

  • Design a responsive e-commerce website that is easy to use and that allows customers to browse through all products and filters by size, color, style, and other.
  • Create a new visual identity for the company that is modern and neutral enough to attract all types of people and styles.

The Basics

This project spanned one month, in which I worked alone and acted as the user experience researcher and the user interface designer.

Pinpointing a Persona

In order to determine the persona that best captured the overall qualities of Mirror's customer-base, I conducted interviews and analyzed competitors with three goals in mind:

  1. Understand why people shop online as opposed to in-store (and vice versa) and empathize with those people.
  2. Demographic of online clothing shopping in general.
  3. Understand what makes Mirror popular and how to translate that when moving online.

I believed that the best methods to achieve these goals were:

  1. Secondary Research to find existing knowledge of the current market;
  2. Competitor Analysis to determine relevant audiences from competitors to facilitate interviews, to better understand the current market, and to consider the existing successes and pain points within e-commerce;
  3. and User Interviews to discover what users like about Mirror and how they feel about online shopping in general.

Results

Defining the Details

Then, to determine what to build out for the wireframes and prototypes, I conducted a card sort that would give me a look into how others' might organize an e-commerce site, a site map to establish site architecture, task flows to provide clarity for essential pages, and a user flow in the persona's POV to see how to appeal specifically to the user-base.

Project Goals

By determining the interests of every party involved and where they intersect, features in the wireframe could be traced back to how that feature might help, enhance, or even hinder goals. To do this, I created a venn diagram.

Site Architecture

I conducted the card sort with 5 participants and was met with surprising insights. Based on those insights and the general sorting pattern, I created the site map.

Task and User Flows

The card sorting and resulting site map gave me a rudimentary idea of what navigating the site might look like. I created the task flows and user flows based on those general impressions.

Building Blueprints

Wireframing

With the basics built out, I moved on to wireframing. Before creating digital lo-fi renderings of Mirror, I sketched a few wireframes of competitor's sites so that I could get a feel for what made for an enjoyable, immersive experience and what didn't. As the goal was to give myself a rough idea of what worked and didn't work, I didn't let sketching take longer than five minutes per drawing.

Because the assignment was to build out a prototype including the three most important pages of the site, I made the product listings and product page when creating higher fidelity wireframes. I included annotations to indicate any micro-interactions that couldn't be included in the wireframes.

I created responsive UIs for each of these page when I was creating the high-fidelity prototype.

UI Kit & Branding

Because one of the goals was to give Mirror a new visual identity alongside its e-commerce debut, I created a new logo and style guide that also informed the UI for the project.

Prototype

Testing the Prototype

With the prototype built out, I approached several more individuals with experience shopping online to test the model. My goal was to evaluate and understand pain points and sources of confusion for users when navigating the website, with the ultimate goal being conversion. Due to COVID-19, I conducted interviews remotely with the screen-recording function of a testing website (Maze) while video-conferencing with the participant in question.

Affinity Map

To organize the results and find patterns that would inform what tweaks to make to iterations, I made an affinity map of the participants' comments.

Next Steps

The assignment ended after discussing hand-off tools, but in a real-life application, these are the things that would happen next:

  • Further refining and testing of the prototype.
  • Hand-off to developers.

Key Takeaways:

There were some things that couldn't be helped given the global circumstances and the academic nature of the assignment (things like number of participants or interview accessibility). I felt throughout the project that my interview/test results could have better informed my decisions throughout the entire process, so I recognize now the value of dedicating massive amounts of time to interviewing and testing during the research processes.
